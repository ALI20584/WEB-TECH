Imran khan

Imran Khan is a prominent Pakistani politician, former cricketer, and the current Prime Minister of Pakistan. He was born on October 5, 1952, in Lahore, Pakistan. Imran Khan became a cricketing sensation during the 1980s, leading the Pakistani cricket team to their first-ever World Cup victory in 1992. After his cricketing career, he ventured into politics and founded the Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf (PTI) political party in 1996. He ran for the Prime Minister's office several times, finally achieving victory in 2018. Imran Khan is known for his strong stance against corruption, his efforts to promote education and health care, and his commitment to a peaceful resolution of conflicts. His leadership has earned him both admirers and critics, and his efforts to reform Pakistan's political system have been a subject of debate and discussion.

Syed Babar Ali

Syed Babar Ali is a renowned Pakistani businessman, philanthropist, and educationist. He was born on 15th June 1926 in Lahore, Pakistan. Syed Babar Ali is the founder of Packages Limited, a leading paper and board manufacturing company in Pakistan, and is considered one of the pioneers of the country's industrialization. He has also played a significant role in promoting education in Pakistan and is the founder and first Chancellor of Lahore University of Management Sciences (LUMS), one of the top universities in the country. Syed Babar Ali has received numerous national and international awards for his contributions to business, education, and philanthropy. His work has been recognized and admired by people from all walks of life, and he continues to inspire generations through his dedication to excellence and service to society.

Liaqat Ali Khan

Liaquat Ali Khan was a prominent Pakistani statesman, lawyer, and the first Prime Minister of Pakistan. He played a vital role in the country's independence movement, working alongside Muhammad Ali Jinnah to secure a separate homeland for Muslims in the Indian subcontinent. As the first Prime Minister of Pakistan, Liaquat Ali Khan made significant efforts to strengthen the country's economy, foreign policy, and defense. He introduced various reforms to promote social justice, education, and health care, and initiated programs to develop infrastructure and industry in the newly formed state. Liaquat Ali Khan also played a crucial role in establishing Pakistan's identity as a democratic state and worked tirelessly to ensure the country's stability and progress.
